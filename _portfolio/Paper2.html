--- title: "Working paper" excerpt: "Freemium is Premium: The Benefits of Free Apps. A Case Study of Steam Videogames " collection: portfolio --- "Thousands of games that don’t break through the market bring in little or nothing, while big free games easily bring in over $100 million a year. During the pandemic, money was coming in by the bucketful ...". Talking about mobile games, this statement was made by a videogame professional during an interview with a journalist from Lapresse.ca, and illustrates the public growing interest on the free apps business model. Providers of free-access apps are known to make money from monetization schemes which may involve in-app spending (microtransactions, downloadable contents, premium subscriptions) made by consumers for upgrading the quality of the app; or additional revenues per user coming from advertisers. This freemium business model is valid for digital videogames, especially for Steam videogames, 15 to 20% of which are free-to-play. In a comparison with pay-to-access apps, revenues per user coming from monetization may be different for these free-to-play apps. This paper is an attempt to estimate that difference in the additional revenue from monetization between free-to-play and pay-to-play apps, along with the differences in their costs. Using online-gathered data from Steam API and SteamSpy, I intend to estimate a freemium pricing decision model for single agent firms that benefit from both the adoption by consumers and the monetization.
