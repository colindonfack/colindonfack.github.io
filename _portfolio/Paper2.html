--- title: "Working paper" excerpt: "Demand analysis with interval-valued sales" collection: portfolio ---
"Most of existing empirical demand studies assume that market shares are fully observed, at least for some products. The issue of partial observation often raised by empiricists occur when zero sales are recorded for few or many products of interest. However, market sales can still be partially observed when they're recorded in interval values, as it is often seen in the recent and growing industry for apps where downloads/installs data may be disclosed by app providers in intervals (e.g. 100+ downloads, 1000+, 10k+, 100k+ downloads). Interval-valued market sales can also be derived when aggregate market shares are observed. This paper is an attempt to use recent developments in partial identification methods to run empirical demand analysis when observed sales are interval-valued. Simulations show that using the middle of intervals to run point estimation may lead to systematic bias; even if the sign of the bias depends on the DGP. An illustration is done in a conditional logit framework using data on recent casual videogames available on the Steam Platform."
